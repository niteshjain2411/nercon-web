Abstracts Guidelines

Submission guidance for original research, case reports, and poster presentation review.

Abstracts must be original, related to Pathology / Diagnostics and should not have been published elsewhere.

  • The presenting author is required to ensure that the co-authors are aware & approve of the content.
  • He/she should take responsibility for the accuracy of the results.
  • The presenting author's name should be highlighted. His/her full name, institution, contact number and email address must be provided.
  • An e-mail confirming the receipt of abstract, including an abstract number, will be sent to the submitting delegate. This number should be quoted for all further correspondence.
  • Acceptance/rejection will be notified by email along with the guidelines for poster preparation.
  • Selected abstracts will be presented as E-Posters.
  • Selected E-Posters will be selected for podium presentation during the conference.
  • Scientific Committee reserves the right to accept/reject your abstract based on scientific merit.
  • For original research studies, the abstract should consist of the following sections: Background, Research Question, Methods, Results and Conclusion. Word limit: 250 words.
  • For case reports & case series the abstract should have a Background, Case Description, and the Learning Points as Conclusions. Word limit: 250 words.
